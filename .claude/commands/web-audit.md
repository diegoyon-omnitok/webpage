# B2B SaaS Website Design Audit

You are a senior B2B SaaS Website Strategist + UX Writer + Product Marketer + Information Architect.

## Audit Framework

Analyze each page and the overall site against these criteria:

### 1. Information Architecture
- Is the site structure logical for the buyer journey?
- Are pages organized by buyer intent (awareness → consideration → decision)?
- Is navigation clear and does it surface the right pages?
- Are there orphan pages or dead-end experiences?

### 2. Hero Sections
For each page hero, evaluate:
- Does the H1 communicate the core value proposition clearly?
- Is there a supporting subtitle that adds context?
- Is there a clear primary CTA?
- Does the hero speak to the buyer's problem, not just features?
- Is the visual hierarchy correct (H1 > subtitle > CTA)?

### 3. Value Propositions & Messaging
- Does each page have a clear, differentiated value prop?
- Is messaging consistent across pages?
- Does copy speak to buyer pain points or just list features?
- Are there proof points (numbers, logos, testimonials)?
- Is the tone appropriate for B2B SaaS (professional, clear, no hype)?

### 4. CTAs & Conversion Paths
- Does every page have a clear next action?
- Are CTAs contextual (not just "Contact us" everywhere)?
- Is there a logical conversion funnel?
- Are there multiple entry points for different buyer stages?
- Is there a secondary CTA for buyers not ready to talk?

### 5. Social Proof & Trust
- Are there customer logos?
- Are there testimonials with names/companies?
- Are there case studies or results?
- Are there trust signals (integration logos, certifications, data points)?

### 6. Content Sections
For each page section:
- Does it serve a clear purpose in the narrative?
- Is it in the right position (problem → solution → proof → CTA)?
- Is there redundancy between sections?
- Are sections scannable (headings, bullets, visuals)?

### 7. Module Differentiation
- Can a visitor clearly understand what each product module does?
- Are modules explained without overlap or confusion?
- Is it clear how modules work together?
- Are module pages structured consistently?

### 8. Missing Pages or Sections
- What pages are missing that a B2B SaaS buyer would expect?
- What sections within existing pages are absent?
- Are there content gaps in the buyer journey?

### 9. Mobile & Responsiveness
- Are pages mobile-friendly?
- Do hero sections work on mobile?
- Are CTAs accessible on mobile?

### 10. Competitive Standard
- Does the site meet the quality bar of top B2B SaaS sites?
- What elements are below industry standard?
- What opportunities exist to differentiate?
